Game Six
HDADTJ Vs Clint Hays’ (Julien Riviere’s) (2226) MYWHL
You’ve seen the deck lists. This deck was designed to rape mains and toys and that’s exactly what it did to me. That, coupled with the fact that I didn’t flip for 6 turns because I couldn’t draw a Vader, or I couldn’t get a battleground for him to go to means an easy win for him. I drew up when Clint had 10 cards on his racer, 12 in his hand, and 6+ cards in his lost pile.
FL -23
10(+91)

Highs: It only took about 7 minutes to play.
Lows: AFA should only work if they occupy a battleground.

I finish in first. Josh Bader from Colorado got second. Then Chu was one differential ahead of Clint for third. Chu beats Josh by one card, cause he’s that damned good, and I play Clint.

Semi Finals
WYS Vs Clint’s Senate
Same as before. Vader got his @#$% smacked, and I just controlled the board (Menace Fades, Battle Plan, Goo Nee Tay, etc). I didn’t care about differential, so I just played to win.
FW +17

The prize money was weak and Chu and I discovered that even if he won first he and Clint weren’t going to break even (plane tickets, hotel etc). We both knew that our light decks would win, but his light would have won by more (probably) so rather than forcing him to risk losing any more money I agreed to give him the first place cash.

That night we all just kinda hung out. I got Juz into L5R. Helped Jay Webb Build decks for the open.

10/10: Wednesday. A monthly tournament at the Gamer’s Edge in Phoenix. Haven’t played since DPC Phoenix. Been busy juggling Katie and Allie, so I build my dark in the car on the drive up with Enrique, Justin Beal, Bobby Dayton, and Pat Gimber. I get to ride in the trunk of the Blazer. Suck. Bobby has to piss like a racehorse since he drank like 1.5 liters of Dr. Pepper before we left. Much laughter ensues as we declare that pit stops are determined not by the one with weakest bladder, but by the one with the strongest, as dictated by the code of man. Rating is 2278. Decks are WYS SOLID and Martin Falke’s second version of his Senate, both with slight modifications.

Game One
Falke Senate Vs Jon Dunn’s (1785) HB Operatives
Lots of senators, lots of squabbles and yet I don’t flip for 230597823048 turns. Literally. Third turn I get Maul in his ship to Kirdo III, which is good. I beat up EPP Luke with Palp (who he failed to kill), Dr. E & Ponda, IG with Gun and an I Have You Now for eight, I think. Then Thrawn + Chimaera + Lat damage on lone Wedge in ship seals it.
FW + 19

Highs: I like John. He silly. Deck fun.
Lows: Kinda non-interactive.

Game Two
WYS Vs Robert Dayton’s (1644) Maul Hut/ JP Start
Bobby is seriously one of the best players I have encountered out here. If he was more competitive I’m sure he could easily be rated at least 2000. We’ve played this match up a couple times in testing, so we knew each other’s decks to a t (what does is that supposed to mean, anyway?) and we both knew I was going to win. To be a great sport, he drew up to give me the full win. Mad Props for that, bud.
FW + 22

Highs: Its cool playing Bobby.
Lows: Playing one another hurt our "team" from Tucson.

Game Three
Falke Senate Vs Robert Dayton’s (1644) TIGIH
Again, I know his deck inside and out and I know how to beat it, but I play like a complete @#$% wipe. DTF/ First Strike did more damage to me than anything all game. Eventually Vader took Luke and hid on the Executor DB, and ZiMH drained at Kashyyyk for the win.
FW+ 7

Highs: Bleh.
Lows: I suck at this game.

WYS Vs Justin Beal’s (1751) Droid AOBS
I can satisfy Battle Plan and he can’t. I am activating more than he is. We skirmish, but I always come out on top. We waste a lot of time trying to figure out how much he can lose by and still come in second as I play with my Edges, and time is called and I find myself with the timed win. My 21 cards to his 2.
TW + 21

Highs: None.
