In its current form, this deck has lost one game for me since it was built, and that was to Brian Hunter’s WYS in the Houston DPC final. Props to Hayes Hunter, Jeremy Losee, Paul Myers, and Brian for advice with it along the way.

It’s Hunt Down. You should know how to play it. This version works best by getting a quick Vader down to pressure them into coming after him, cancelling the battle, then retaliating with unbridled fury. In almost every game I play with this deck, I cause 10+ overflow battle damage at one point or another, and it’s not difficult to do. You always start IAO & Secret Plans (aka the best card in STAR WARS...hehe) and YCHF & Mobilization Points. The third effect depends on what you’re going up against, which I will elaborate on in the matchups section.

At the start of the game, your primary objective is of course to get Vader down as quickly as possible in order to make Visage an immediate problem for your opponent. You want to try to throw them off their gameplan by causing so much damage that it cannot be ignored. Then, you deflect their offensive and spring your trap into action by dropping crippling beatdowns. A fairly simple concept, eh?

CARD CHOICES:

Blockade Flagship: Docking Bay - +8 to all of opponent’s transitting here is huge. This often is responsible for the trapped EPP that gets taken for massive overflow. It’s a mobile site, so Crush The Rebellion limits their destiny and the site is immune to POAS/LITW. I don’t play the CC DB simply because Stunning Leader may not be played there.

Non-combo Ghhhk - I play a Masterful Move, hence no Ghhhk combo.

Stunning Leader x2 - Not only would the deck get owned by LS Senate without them, but they can be key in other matchups in which you find yourself having to stop EPP attacks (which is a lot of matchups).

Bad Feeling Have I x2 - This card is bad news for just about any deck these days. Two copies ensures it makes an earlier and thus more devastating appearance.

Colo Claw Fish - A useful card in many matchups thanks to the floating extra Visages, but it also can be used to simply deny your opponent from using it, which is key against Senate.

CARDS THAT SOMETIMES MAKE THE CUT:

Shut Him Up Or Shut Him Down - Cancel Bacta Tank against WYS/Senate.

Kintan Strider - A nifty card that can be pulled with Masterful Move. A quasi-Jedi’s Resilience that is lower destiny, but works with any character.

After Her! - Easy retrieval for Hunt Down? I’m interested.

They’re Still Coming Through! - I used this at the DPC, but from what I understand, this card cannot be played if you have no valid target to redirect the Portal to. Thus Patrol Crafts are problematic.

P-59 - Solid character that gets some use against Raiders.

Prince Xizor - Also good against WYS.

Grand Moff Tarkin - Still the only safe way to prevent Fallen Portal.

MATCHUPS:

WYS - Start Crush The Rebellion for your third effect so that access to I Have You Now is instant. Non-Raider versions of WYS are nothing to worry about. Get Vader to Tatooine quickly, and look for your ships. Drain for a lot and attack them on both fronts. A Useless Gesture sends TT out of play, so Visage is as powerful against WYS as it is against other non-mains decks. I have never lost to a non-Raider WYS deck with Hunt Down. Against Raiders, be cautious. Luke will almost positively make an appearance inside a Patrol Craft to flip you back and stop the Visage damage, so make a run with EPP Maul or Sidious and IHYN to get him off the table. The quick jump you get is what is most important. If it takes them too long to find Luke, or you kill him as soon as he hits the table, then you have a great chance of depleting them with drains/Visage before they can get the lockdown established. Fallen Portal, Bacta Tank, and the Patrol Crafts make this a difficult matchup, but a few adjustments can certainly be made to make it less of a hassle. This deck is 1-0 against non-Raider WYS; 1-1 against WYS Raiders.

QMC - I would suggest starting CTR in this matchup for the Monnok/IHYN and the destiny-limiting. This is one of the easier matchups for the deck, but it can be a close game. A quick Vader and BFHI along with a bit of denial (give them the Carida icon only) will give this deck a huge headache. It could take a while until they run out of avoidance cards, but the beatdown should manifest eventually. In both of my tourney games against QMC, a beatdown (one for 29 overflow, the other for 18) caught them and essentially ended the game in both contests. Meanwhile, Stunning Leader helps to thwart any offensives they attempt. If they get too strong a hold at Bespin for you to disrupt, then toss a ship or two to Carida so that Battle Plan isn’t a factor if the game drags out.

Senate - Use your third effect for Colo Claw Fish every time. This matchup appears difficult on paper, but this deck is 2-0 against Senate in tourney games. The quick BFHI is key, since you give them only 2 icons (Carida and the Blockade DB). Get Vader with someone else to the DB quickly, and let Visage pressure them into attacking you (which they’ll do regardless). It’s very rare that they can afford to take two cards from your hand, deploy characters, battle, pay 1 to retrieve w/ DTF, and use Palpy all in the same turn. More often than not, they will pass on taking two from your hand and just deploy their EPPs to kill your Vader. Use Stunning Leader or the Ghhhk to stop the beatdown, and then retaliate with Fett/Maul/etc. The HDADTJ objective puts a huge dent in their Sense/Alter capabilities, so your Stunning Leader should go through 9 times out of 10. Be safe and keep your hand size somewhat large to shrink the possibility of them taking the Stunning Leader/Ghhhk out of your hand, and you should be set. Chiraneau can offer additional pressure, but keep an Imperial Command in hand so that they can’t end the game with Life Debt.

Combat - I generally start No Escape against LSC, because Honor is both common and potent in this match. The (V) I Find Your Lack Of Faith Disturbing turned this matchup around. Instead of them being able to stack two 7s and off your Dark Jedi without hassle each turn, they have to draw a destiny blindly, which cuts their advantage in half. When your Dark Jedi are on the table after their move phase, they MUST have an avoidance card each turn to survive. Get Janus on the table, and recycle Fett if you have to (by losing 2 to send him to Used Pile) and bring the hammer each turn. Most Combat decks pack 3x Dodge, so if they draw a large hand, Monnok lost could mean big problems for them. Chiraneua/Chimaera should hit Carida for the drain of 2, unless you are using your space resources to kill their Virtual Luke or something. Most of these decks use Racing, and some are Shield Busters. You have built-in SAC protection along with the Odds killer among your starting cards, so you aren’t in too much trouble against those decktypes.

HB Flip - You can pitch the Mob Points combo for Search And Destroy quickly if you want (unless they make a point to get the DDTA shield out after you pull Carida). Get Vader down quickly, the usual. HB Flip generally doesn’t use more than two copies of Transmission Terminated (or OOC/TT), so Visage gives them cramps all game since you have two backup Visages. I’d probably go ahead and grab the TT to stop them from retrieving it. If S + D hits, you win big. If they deploy characters to the Forest or something to stop it, then you can beatdown with Maul, Dr. E combo, etc. Stunning Leader mocks their suicide EPPs and can get them into a lot of trouble if they aren’t holding a Houjix. During the mid-game, deploy your fleet and probe intelligently. People tell me that HB Flip is good against Hunt Down, but I vehemently disagree. I have NEVER lost a tournament game to HB Flip while using HDADTJ (which adds up to a good chunk of games). Not that that says anything, but I will continue to dismiss the notion that this is a potentially bad matchup.

Throne Room Mains - This deck appears to be making a resurgence, but I have yet to face off against a modern version. Start CTR. They go first, which means you get no Carida or Mob Points activation. This shouldn’t deter your gameplan much, as you still want to get Vader to the table in a speedious manner. You might encounter a stagnant early game if your draw isn’t good, but you won’t be choked to the point where you cannot recover. Use the Evader wisely if needed, and go about playing as if you were facing a normal mains deck. BFHI hinders them as much as Goo does you. This will probably be a close game unless something drastic happens, but if you maintain the upper hand in battling, then Visage would seem to be the deciding factor (which is the case against most mains decks).

Most other decktypes shouldn’t give you anything to worry about.
