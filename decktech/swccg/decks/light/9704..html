    -Why add the Spaceport Docking Bay?
    I chose this over the Cantina mainly because it gives me DB access to any other oddball planets the Dark Side may be playing such as Ralltiir.

    -Why take out the space?
    OK, space isn’t needed in this deck. I’ve played 3 different heavy space decks during playtesting and having space in this deck hurts more than anything. By putting the Home One and Falcon out, you risk severe beatdown. So now this deck concentrates 100% on ground. When playing space just drain like mad, and let the direct damage do its thing.

    -Why Lost In The Wilderness?
    Well, this card is tech. Play the good-old ASP-707/Ewok Catapult combo to drop a 7 destiny on your reserve, play this and make any of the opponent’s characters go missing. I’ve even made Lord Vader get lost at the CC: Landing Platform. Niiiice.

    -Why’d I get rid of Bargaining Table?
    I decided to take it out mainly because there’s a very good chance it may never make it out on the table. And if it did, it would help very little. So I made the decision to go with heavier retrieval to get more cards back. This deck has potential to retrieve up to 40 a game, which isn’t bad at all...

    Version 1.0 Strategy...

    This is a powerful deck that I thought up on my quest for a good, consistant, solid, original, Light Side deck.

    The Play:
    -The goal of this deck is simple: flip Profit as soon as possible, while seting up power at sites with your Eloms and mains. While doing that, get out your docking bays (Endor: Landing Platform first) and drop the ASP droids and Ewok Catapults for easy 7 destinies. The advantages with this combo in this deck are:
    1.) On and Off The Edges. Easy retrieval.
    2.)Fallen Portal. I love this card, but with the ASP/Catapult combo, you can take out Palpy, Vader, you name it. Drop a 7 on your reserve, play Fallen Portal. That’s a total of 9! Sweet tech.
    Eloms and Tessek increase drains drastically, as well as power and retrieval (Nar Shadda Wind Chimes). The last and final element in this deck is direct damage. The Dark Side is losing 5 a turn while flipped. + 2 with Order To Engage. + 2 with Civil Disorder (don’t deploy until mid-late game to get the best effect). That’s that...

    The Cards:
    Objective:
    I use Profit, well, because it’s Profit ;)...

    Locations:
    Location choice is simple and perfectly fits this deck. The Tatooine locations are pretty self-explanatory. Audience Chamber and the Palace to start with. Tatooine Docking Bay to get off Tatooine and for extra activation. Cantina for drains. Cloud City Platform is for Off The Edge retrieval, as well as the Endor: Landing Platform which is used to setup your ASP/Catapult tech.

    Characters:
    Once again simple. 3 Luke’s and 3 Ben’s for backup. EPP Han to replace the crap Han. Boushh is all-around cool. Eloms and Tessek for retreival, power, and drains. Chewbacca Of Kashyyyk cause it’s the only Chewie I have, and well, I need Chewie to get the most out of the flip side. Wedge is to drop on the Falcon with Lando. And ASP droids are for...nevermind, you know.

    Devices:
    If I have to tell you, don’t bother reviewing the deck...


    Interrupts:
    Fallen Portal to kill off the Dark Side’s characters/ On/Off The Edge for retrieval. Someone Who Loves you for an extra way to free Han if you can’t happen to get control of the AC. Rebel Barrier for a bit of protection from beatdown. Nar Shadaa Wind Chimes for retrieval and to grab Eloms when ya need’em.

    Effects:
    Bacta Tank to save characters from lost pile. Bargaining Table for heavy drain protection. Civil Disorder for late game direct damage. Do, Or Do Not...anti-SAC. Honor Of The Jedi rules all. Insurrection to grab sites quick-like. OTE for early game direct damage. Staging Areas for super-activation, and finally Wise Advice...anti-SAC.

    This deck can take on just about anything. ROPS dies to this (Eloms rule!). If you’re playing heavy space, just avoid it all together. Home One and Lando In Falcon are just to take on a bit of space, so don’t try to take on a TIE Swarm deck or anything, you’ll die, trust me. There are some other cards I would have liked to put in but just didn’t have the deck space, such as:

  • R-3PO

  • Your Insight Serves You Well
